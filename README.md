Patient Flow Analysis
Problem

Hospitals often experience inefficiencies at the department level, resulting in increased waiting times and suboptimal use of clinical capacity. This project focuses on identifying operational bottlenecks within departments to improve patient throughput and reduce delays.

Data

The analysis is based on structured, synthetic patient-level data, including:

Patient ID
Department (Akut, Kirurgi, Ambulatorium)
Arrival time
Treatment duration (minutes)
Waiting time (minutes)

The dataset represents patient activity across multiple days and departments, enabling time-based and cross-sectional analysis.

Analyse

We performed exploratory and descriptive analysis to:

Evaluate waiting time distribution across departments
Identify peak arrival periods and demand patterns
Compare treatment duration and throughput
Detect operational bottlenecks at department level
Resultater

Key insights include:

Higher waiting times observed in surgical departments
Clear peak periods with increased patient inflow
Variation in treatment times across departments
Indications of capacity constraints during high-demand hours
Anbefalinger

Based on the findings, we recommend:

Adjusting staffing levels during peak hours
Redistributing patient load where possible
Monitoring waiting times as a key operational KPI
Implementing simple forecasting for demand planning
